# v2.0.4 — 2026-07-21

## Villager Trading

| Tag | Change |
|---:|---|
| ![feature] | <details><summary>Stock-market-driven villager trade repricing</summary><br>Villager and wandering-trader offers for market-listed items are rewritten at interaction time: emerald costs are replaced by the configured trading-currency item, priced from the live stock market with configurable margins (villager buys below market, sells above market). Items without a market keep their normal vanilla emerald trades — offers convert automatically when a market is created and revert when it is deleted.<br><br><ul><li>New <code>VillagerTrading</code> group in <code>settings.json</code>: <code>ENABLED</code> (default <code>true</code>), <code>PRICE_REFRESH_INTERVAL_MINUTES</code> (default <code>20</code> ≈ one Minecraft day), <code>VILLAGER_BUY_MARGIN</code> (default <code>0.8</code>), <code>VILLAGER_SELL_MARGIN</code> (default <code>1.2</code>).</li><li><b>Master-authoritative:</b> only the master server reads the settings; it broadcasts the price table (including the currency item) to all slave servers on a new server-to-server packet, so enabling it on the master enables it everywhere.</li><li>Original emerald offers are stored in world data, so disabling the feature restores every villager's original trades. Item ingredient slots (e.g. book + emeralds for enchanted books) are preserved — only the emerald part becomes currency.</li></ul></details> |
| ![feature] | <details><summary>Pay merchant money costs with any combination of denominations</summary><br>Offers priced in BankSystem money are matched by <b>value</b>, not by exact note type: a 20.00 cost can be paid with 1×20, 2×10, 4×5, or even a single 50 note, split across both payment slots.<br><br><ul><li>Exact combinations are consumed cleanly; overpayment returns exact change (fewest notes) into the payment slots, the inventory, or dropped at the player's feet — no value is ever lost, even when shift-clicking through a whole stack of large notes.</li><li>Clicking an offer row auto-fills the payment slots with money from the inventory, even when the exact denomination shown on the offer is not owned.</li><li>Only genuine BankSystem money is matched by value — renamed/modified notes, other mods' currencies, and non-money offers keep untouched vanilla behavior.</li></ul></details> |

## Performance

| Tag | Change |
|---:|---|
| ![improvement] | <details><summary>Massively reduced CPU usage of the default orderbook volume distribution</summary><br>The plugin that keeps orderbooks filled with baseline volume no longer recomputes its full price distribution for every market on every update — a hot spot that previously ran ~1000 expensive per-price-level calculations per market, per cycle.<br><br><ul><li>Each market's target distribution is now cached and only recomputed when something actually changes: the market price, the visible price range, or the plugin settings (volume scale / default price).</li><li>When several markets do need a refresh, they take turns round-robin — at most one market recomputes its distribution per update cycle, so servers with many markets no longer pay the full cost all at once. Market resets, settings changes, and price-range shifts still refresh immediately.</li><li>The per-price-level computation itself is cheaper: loop-invariant values (current market price, currency scale factor) are fetched once per range instead of once per price level.</li><li><b>Plugin API:</b> new <code>IPluginOrderBook.getDefaultRawVolume(backendStartPrice, count)</code> batch method lets plugins sample a contiguous price range in one cheap call instead of ~1000 single-price calls.</li></ul>While prices are static the heavy math does not run at all; only a lightweight convergence loop remains per update.</details> |

## Plugins

| Tag | Change |
|---:|---|
| ![feature] | <details><summary>VolatilityPlugin: player trading now permanently shifts market prices (economy self-balancing)</summary><br>The plugin's random walk no longer anchors on the static default price — it follows a <b>price equilibrium</b> driven by cumulative player trading. Sustained net player selling lowers a market's price range; net buying raises it. The shift is persistent and self-balancing: heavily farmed items get cheaper, heavily consumed items get more expensive.<br><br><ul><li>The impact scales with the item's <b>Abundance</b> — common items absorb more trading before their price moves.</li><li>The exponential impact curve means the price approaches but can never reach 0; additionally, the equilibrium is clamped between configurable min/max multiples of the default price.</li><li>Four new per-market GUI-editable settings: <b>Player Flow Influence</b> (on by default), <b>Flow Sensitivity</b> (default <code>500</code>), <b>Min Price Multiplier</b> (default <code>0.05</code>), <b>Max Price Multiplier</b> (default <code>20</code>).</li><li>Disable the Player Flow Influence toggle or reset the market's <b>Net Flow</b> to return to the old anchored-on-default-price behavior.</li><li>Existing worlds keep working — old saved plugin settings load with the new defaults.</li></ul></details> |
| ![feature] | <details><summary>Plugin performance profiler bar in the Plugin Management window</summary><br>A stacked timing bar across the top of the <b>Plugin Management</b> window shows per-plugin, per-pass execution time relative to the server tick budget, so admins can see at a glance which plugin/pass is spending how much of a tick — and whether the installed plugin set fits inside the budget.<br><br><ul><li>Full width represents the <b>effective plugin budget</b> — 50&nbsp;ms minus non-plugin server work (derived from the tick pre→post gap), so mods that also spend tick time shrink the bar's headroom accordingly.</li><li>Each plugin contributes one <b>Normal update</b> segment plus one <b>Finalisation stage</b> segment, drawn end-to-end in a stable per-plugin colour (deterministic from the plugin's UUID); the finalisation pass uses a slightly darker shade of the same colour so passes stay associated with their plugin.</li><li>Hovering a segment shows a tooltip with plugin name, pass label, and latest / rolling-average / peak execution time, plus % of the tick budget consumed and the plugin's subscribed-market count.</li><li>Segments never draw past the bar's max width; when the combined <b>enabled</b>-plugin average exceeds the effective budget a <b>red overflow marker</b> and warning line appear, telling the admin the plugin load is too high for the server tick.</li><li>Disabled plugins are drawn dimmed toward mid-grey and excluded from the overload check. Empty state, single plugin, and overflow all render cleanly.</li><li>The feed is delivered as an S2C stream at 500&nbsp;ms cadence, admin- and trust-gated (mirrors the plugin runtime-data stream); untrusted slaves and non-admin clients see an empty bar and no data flows.</li></ul></details> |

## News Event System

| Tag | Change |
|---:|---|
| ![feature] | <details><summary>Craftable newspaper and market-moving news events</summary><br>A new <b>NewsPlugin</b> randomly publishes news events — headlines that temporarily (or permanently) move the prices of the affected markets. Players read the feed through a craftable <b>newspaper item</b>, also openable from the Trade and Management screens and via <code>/stockmarket news</code>.<br><br><ul><li>Events are defined in JSON files under <code>config/StockMarket/news/</code>; eight default events ship out of the box, and a documented schema lets admins author their own.</li><li>Each event has a category (shock, trend, crash, permanent shift, …), a per-market impact envelope, and a headline/body shown in the newspaper.</li><li>An <b>opt-in popup toast</b> notifies interested players the moment news breaks; players who were offline get a catch-up toast on join.</li><li>The plugin exposes per-market settings (news enabled, sensitivity) and multiplies the volatility target price in a separate finalize stage, so headlines translate into real, bot-driven price movement regardless of plugin order.</li></ul></details> |

## Security & Multi-Server Trust

| Tag | Change |
|---:|---|
| ![hardening] | <details><summary>Untrusted-slave orders / management ops are now blocked instead of processed</summary><br>Previously the "trusted slave" flag from BankSystem was signalling only — the master accepted and matched orders from any connected slave regardless of trust. Now every mutating request is rejected at the master before it touches the orderbook, and the affected UI on the slave-side client reflects the state so players see it before clicking.<br><br><ul><li><b>Server-side gate:</b> new <code>NetworkGate.isMutatingCallAllowed(...)</code> helper consults BankSystem's <code>ServerBankManager.isSlaveServerTrusted(slaveID)</code> and is wired into 14 mutating request handlers — create/cancel orders (incl. inter-market), all plugin management (create, delete, reorder, settings, custom settings, subscribe/unsubscribe), preset updates, player-prefs writes, mod-settings <code>SET</code>, and admin news ops. Read-only requests (market list, prices, history, orderbook depth, news feed, player prefs read, mod-settings <code>GET</code>) stay open so untrusted slaves remain browsable. The <code>AsyncMarket</code> forwarding path also gained an <code>isAllowedToCallByUntrustedSlaveServer</code> override that denies <code>SetMarketOpen</code>, <code>SetSettings</code>, and <code>ResetNetPlayerItemFlow</code>.</li><li><b>Client-side awareness:</b> a new <code>SlaveTrustCache</code> populates on the slave from BankSystem's authoritative check, delivered via a new S2C <code>SlaveTrustSyncPacket</code> so every connected client learns "am I on a trusted slave?" without reconnecting. <code>ClientSettings.isSlaveTrusted()</code> exposes it to the GUI layer.</li><li><b>Client UI:</b> on an untrusted slave the trade terminal, plugin management, preset editor, and create-market screens render an inline three-line info banner and disable their mutating buttons/inputs. The Management screen refuses to open entirely — the player gets a chat message pointing them at <code>/banksystem trust &lt;slaveID&gt;</code>. Read-only browsing continues to work as before.</li><li><b>Runtime propagation:</b> when an admin runs <code>/banksystem trust</code> or <code>/banksystem untrust</code> at runtime, connected clients on the affected slave transition to the correct UI state within a tick — no reconnect required. Driven by a new <code>TrustFlagUpdatePacket</code> (master→slave) that consumes a matching BankSystem event.</li></ul>Two new lang keys (<code>gui.stockmarket.untrusted_slave.banner_line1</code>/<code>line2</code>/<code>line3</code> and <code>.tooltip</code>, plus <code>.management_blocked</code>) added in <code>en_us</code>, <code>de_de</code>, and <code>de_ch</code>.</details> |
| ![hardening] | <details><summary>Management-data reads and the live plugin feed withheld from untrusted slaves</summary><br>Untrusted slave servers could still <i>read</i> sensitive management data even though their writes were blocked. The master now also refuses management-data reads — the plugin list, plugin settings and custom settings, presets, and mod settings — as well as the continuous plugin runtime data stream, when the requesting slave is not trusted. Ordinary market browsing (prices, history, orderbook depth, news feed) stays open, so untrusted slaves remain read-only for players while their admin surface is closed.</details> |
| ![hardening] | <details><summary>Plugin, preset and mod-settings access now requires the StockMarket-admin flag</summary><br>Access to the management surface previously relied on operator level alone. Every plugin, preset, and mod-settings read, write, and the live runtime feed now require the dedicated StockMarket-admin permission flag, so operator level by itself no longer grants access to market administration.</details> |
| ![hardening] | The clear-order-history operation, previously ungated, now passes through the same access-control checks as the other mutating management operations. |
| ![hardening] | <details><summary>Order placement requires full trading permission on the funding account</summary><br>Placing an order — both regular and inter-market — now requires the player to hold <b>both</b> Withdraw and Deposit permission on the target bank account, for either trade direction. Previously a buy checked only Deposit and a sell only Withdraw. Personal-account orders are additionally verified against the sender's own UUID, so a crafted client can no longer target another player's personal account.<br><br><b>Behavior change:</b> a shared-account member holding only one of the two permissions can no longer trade from that account.</details> |
| ![bugfix] | Admins connected through a trusted slave server could not reach the management surface because a permission check only recognised the local player; the check now works for admins on trusted slaves as intended. |
